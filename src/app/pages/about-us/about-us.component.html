1. About Polifacto

Polifacto . in is a platform designed for the Indian audience, offering a space to evaluate and engage with statements and promises made by politicians and political parties. Polifacto aggregates relevant sources from across the internet to provide citizens with the necessary information.

At Polifacto, we empower users with information sources and the necessary context to form their own opinions. Users can then express their perspectives by participating in our interactive rating system, reflecting their understanding and insights. Importantly, we do not make judgments or offer conclusions—our role is to present the relevant details and enable users to make informed decisions based on their own understanding. Through community-driven engagement and a user-led rating system, Polifacto fosters transparency and accountability, encouraging active participation in shaping a more informed democracy.

2. How do we evaluate?

The User Rating System is an interactive feature designed to empower users to evaluate and rate statements and promises made by politicians and political parties. This system provides users with access to relevant information and sources about a particular statement or promise, allowing them to understand the context and form their own informed opinions.

Using this system, users can assess the authenticity, progress, or fulfillment of statements and promises based on their interpretation of the provided details. By clicking on the rating options available on the platform, users can express their perspectives, contributing to a collective understanding and community-driven evaluation of political claims.

At Polifacto, we take a meticulous approach to selecting content, focusing on relevance and the latest developments in the political landscape. Our goal is to address the most significant political statements and claims with impartiality and objectivity. Here's how we do it:

Sources of Content

  • User Submissions: We verify content submitted by users who ask questions or request fact-checks.
  • Political Statements: We select statements made by politicians in speeches, interviews, and debates.
  • Promises and Manifestos: We add promises and policy agendas presented by political parties.
  • Social Media: We focus on individual statements found on platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram.

Our selection of statements for verification ultimately depends on the availability and quality of content, and will never be perfect. We therefore welcome suggestions and criticisms for evaluation and selection.

At Polifacto, we evaluate statements using a structured approach to ensure clarity and transparency. Each statement is categorized into one of the following four categories:

  1. False
    • Claims that contradict publicly available figures or information about actions or inactions.
    • Statements where the methods of calculation differ significantly, and the claim is not supported by any reliable source.
  2. Truth
    • Statements that use accurate information in the correct context.
    • You may use "qualified truth" for cases where the statement has minor inaccuracies or small deviations (e.g., numerical data within a 10% margin of error).
    • Rounding is tolerated unless the politician specifically emphasizes precise accuracy.
  3. Misleading
    • Statements that use correct facts but are presented in a way that misleads the audience.
    • Typically, these involve:
      • Statements taken out of context.
      • Distorted comparisons or other manipulations that could lead to a false conclusion.
    • It is not a middle ground between truth and falsehood but rather an intentional misuse of factual data.
  4. No comment
    • This category includes statements which you might not want to form an opinion about.

Our goal is to provide unbiased and transparent information and sources, empowering you to form informed opinions to better understand political claims and their context.

At Polifacto, we believe that political accountability starts with evaluating promises made by parties and leaders. To ensure clarity and transparency, Polifacto has provided a platform where you can mark the promises as Fullfilled, Violated, In Progress,Not Implemented, Partly Implemented or Not Verified Yet based on your own opinions and understanding. The goal of this feature is to provide a collective perspective about the promises, while empowering you to decide the outcome for yourself. This helps everyone to stay informed about the commitments made during elections and the extent to which they have been honored.

Our Approach

We provide the manifestos of political parties competing in the election. These manifestos outline the promises and commitments made to the public. Each promise can be categorized into one of the following five categories:

  1. Fulfilled: Fully implemented promises that meet the original commitment.
  2. Violated: Promises disregarded or contradicted by opposing actions.
  3. In Progress: Promises currently under active implementation but not yet completed.
  4. Not Implemented: Promises for which no visible steps or actions have been taken.
  5. Partly Implemented: Promises partially fulfilled, with significant portions unaddressed.
  6. Not Verified Yet: This category includes promises awaiting updates, independent audits,
    or further implementation steps for conclusive evaluation.

Why This Matters

By providing this platform with these categories of promises, we want you to have a clear picture of how political parties perform against their own commitments. Your evaluations empower citizens to make informed decisions, hold leaders accountable, and promote transparency in democratic processes.

At Polifacto, our mission is to bridge the gap between promises and delivery, fostering a culture of trust and accountability in Indian politics.
