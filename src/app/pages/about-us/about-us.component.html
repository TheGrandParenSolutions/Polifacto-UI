1. About Polifacto

Polifacto . in is a unique Indian project that focuses on verifying the statements of politicians, disinformation and other false content on social networks.

At Polifacto.in, we aim to highlight false and misleading expressions that distort public discourse. We focus on evaluating the factual statements and declarations of Indian politicians, subjecting them to rigorous scrutiny. Through our work, we aim to inform the public about when political leaders are being truthful and when they may be misleading the people they serve. By shedding light on inaccuracies and manipulations, we help foster transparency, accountability, and a more informed citizenry. Our project strives to protect the integrity of public discourse and ensure that facts remain at the forefront of democratic dialogue.

2. How do we evaluate?

Fact-checking is the process of verifying the accuracy and truthfulness of factual statements, particularly those made by public figures such as politicians. It involves examining claims that can be substantiated with evidence, including numerical data, documented actions (such as voting records or public statements), and historical events. The goal of fact-checking is to ensure transparency and accountability by distinguishing between truthful information and misleading or false assertions.

At Polifacto, we carefully select content to verify based on relevance, balance, and verifiability. Our goal is to ensure impartiality and objectivity while addressing the most significant political statements and claims. Here's how we do it:

Sources of Content

  • User Submissions: We verify content submitted by users who ask questions or request fact-checks.
  • Political Statements: We analyze statements made by politicians in speeches, interviews, and debates.
  • Promises and Manifestos: We examine promises and policy agendas presented by political parties.
  • Social Media: We focus on individual statements found on platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram.

Our selection of statements for verification ultimately depends on the availability and quality of content, and will never be perfect. We therefore welcome suggestions and criticisms for evaluation and selection.

At Polifacto, we evaluate statements using a structured approach to ensure clarity and transparency. Each statement is categorized into one of the following four categories:

  1. False
    • Claims that contradict publicly available figures or information about actions or inactions.
    • Statements where the methods of calculation differ significantly, and the claim is not supported by any reliable source.
  2. Truth
    • Statements that use accurate information in the correct context.
    • We may use "qualified truth" for cases where the statement has minor inaccuracies or small deviations (e.g., numerical data within a 10% margin of error).
    • Rounding is tolerated unless the politician specifically emphasizes precise accuracy.
  3. Misleading
    • Statements that use correct facts but are presented in a way that misleads the audience.
    • Typically, these involve:
      • Statements taken out of context.
      • Distorted comparisons or other manipulations that could lead to a false conclusion.
    • It is not a middle ground between truth and falsehood but rather an intentional misuse of factual data.
  4. Unverified
    • This category includes statements awaiting updates, independent audits,
      or further implementation steps for conclusive evaluation.

Our goal is to provide unbiased and transparent evaluations, empowering users with accurate information to better understand political claims and their context.

At Polifacto, we believe that political accountability starts with evaluating promises made by parties and leaders. To ensure clarity and transparency, we use a structured approach to analyze promises based on their outcomes and progress. This helps citizens stay informed about the commitments made during elections and the extent to which they have been honored.

Our Approach

We begin by reviewing the manifestos of political parties competing in the election. These manifestos outline the promises and commitments made to the public. Each promise is then evaluated thoroughly and categorized into one of the following five categories:

  1. Fulfilled: Fully implemented promises that meet the original commitment.
  2. Violated: Promises disregarded or contradicted by opposing actions.
  3. In Progress: Promises currently under active implementation but not yet completed.
  4. Not Implemented: Promises for which no visible steps or actions have been taken.
  5. Partly Implemented: Promises partially fulfilled, with significant portions unaddressed.
  6. Not Verified Yet: This category includes promises awaiting updates, independent audits,
    or further implementation steps for conclusive evaluation.

Why This Matters

By categorizing promises, we provide a clear picture of how political parties perform against their own commitments. Our evaluations empower citizens to make informed decisions, hold leaders accountable, and promote transparency in democratic processes.

At Polifacto, our mission is to bridge the gap between promises and delivery, fostering a culture of trust and accountability in Indian politics. Explore our promise evaluations and join us in building a more informed and engaged electorate.
